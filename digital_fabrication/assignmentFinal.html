The Press Fit

Bill of Materials

  • The Phone Itself.
  • 1-3 sheets (always good to have extra) of 3mm thick plywood at least 12" by 24'
  • Laser Cutter, 10W Diode
  • Calipers!

Source Files

CAD File Scalable Vector Graphic (SVG) Adobe Illustrator File LightBurn File

Instructions

  1. ALWAYS prioritize safety when using your laser cutter. Clean your laser, check for debris around the bed, make sure your glasses are on/near, check exhaust, have fire safety equiptment around
  2. Double check for glasses, enclosure, and proper exhaust
  3. Do a meterial test for the wood panels. We want the laser to cut through the material completely.
  4. Use the calipers to measure the materials.
  5. If you're modifying these instructions for your own phone, hop into the CAD file and see the progression I used. I first modeled the case, then removed cross sections (think about tolerances!), laid pieces flat, and projected to CPlane/made flat.
  6. If you're using The Phone Itself., then import the above SVG into your laser cutter software, use the settings appropriate for your machine.
  7. Transfer your GCODE to the laser cutter by direct connection or SD, start your laser cutter, and (after putting on your glasses, starting the exhaust, and preparing your closure) press start!
  8. Material varies. You may need to adjust and reprint: use calipers to diagnose erroneous assumptions!

The Box Joint - Acrylic

Bill of Materials

  • The Phone Itself.
  • A 10W Diode Laser (or better - CO2 Laser would do any color acrylic, diodes struggle with most, but should be able to handle redder hues)
  • 5-10 sheets of preferred color
  • Calipers

Source Files

CAD file SVG Adobe Illustrator

Instructions

  1. ALWAYS prioritize safety when using your laser cutter. Clean your laser, check for debris around the bed, make sure your glasses are on/near, check exhaust, have fire safety equiptment around
  2. Double check #1.
  3. If you're modifying the files for your own phone, hop into the CAD file, and follow the steps to build a good aproximation for your case.
  4. Start out by designing with tolerance in mind. See this link for exercises to think about tolerances. I know my tolerances for acrylic are complicated because acrylic thickness varies - I like to assume a median of the thickest and thinnest measurements of my material. So if my acrylics thickest width is 3mm and the thinnest is 2.4, I aim for design for 2.7. Working with tolerances in mind, I then add .15mm to opposing sides. What this means: one panel will use 2.7 knobs. All panels attached to this will use 2.85.
  5. Run a test. Using a little of your acrylic, make a small cube of your draft and run it. Adjust your assumptions for your next test run.
  6. Exort your design as SVG. Depending on your methods, you may need to stop by Adobe Illustrator or Inkscape to clean up some lines. You should have no lines other than the borders of each panel.
  7. Import your SVG into your laser cutting software, and press start!
  8. Once you feel comfortable, increase the complexity of your design. When you're ready, (you'll know) start printing all panels.
  9. Put together your phone case --sliding The Phone Itself. inside before sealing it up!

The Casted - Wax - Limited Edition

Bill of Materials

  • The Phone Itself.
  • Wax
  • Wick, about 9"
  • wick base
  • optional: coloring or fragrance
  • 3D Printer
  • Filament of Choice

Source Files

CAD File

Instructions

  1. If you're creating cases for your own phone, grab your dimensions or your version of the The Phone Itself
    2. , and hop into the CAD file to adjust.
  2. Export your CAD to stl, send her to print.
  3. When your print is done, begin heating up a small amount of beeswax, about 2oz.
  4. Cut and assemble your wick.Do not place it in the mold yet.
  5. With the melted beeswax, pour into the half way point of the of the mold with the top removed.
  6. As it cools, place the wick in so it floats just at the top
  7. BE CAREFUL ON THIS STEP-- IF YOU PRESS TOO HARD< YOU WILL BURN YOURSELF like I did. After the layer has further cooled, grab your The Phone Itself. and gently (slowly) push the phone into the layer.
  8. Snap the top of the mold into place while you heat up more wax and add any colors or fragrances you want.
  9. SUGGESTION: I put a litte bit of masking tap over my phone "buttons" and holes which I removed later.
  10. Once the wax is melted again, very slowly pour small amounts around your Phone Itself.
  11. Let this cool for about 1 hour or less. It's important to remove the candle when it's still warm-ish/moldable.
  12. Remove! Light it up and enjoy.

The Folded

Bill of Materials

  • The Phone Itself.
  • 4-100 (always have extra) 11 x 8.5" piece of card stock
  • 4-100 8 x 6" piece of card stock
  • Laser Cutter 10W Diode minimum

Source Files

11 x 8.5" lightburn file 8 x 6 lightburn file

Instructions

  1. ALWAYS prioritize safety when using your laser cutter. Clean your laser, check for debris around the bed, make sure your glasses are on/near, check exhaust, have fire safety equiptment around
  2. NOTE: This project was constructed entirely in lightburn, so there isn't an SVG or similar.
  3. First do your material test on both sizes of paper with speed and power. You want the slowest speed and lowest power that will cut thorugh both AND the lowest and slowest that will simply allow for creasing without cutting.
  4. If you're using your own phone, grab your dimensions or The Phone Itself. and adjust the file.
  5. When ready, convert to GCODE and connect to the laser cutter. You should print each of these separately to ensure proper centering.
  6. Remove the paper and follow instructions here to fold your paper into a top and bottom.
  7. Ta Da!
